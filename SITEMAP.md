# Sitemap — Studio Jeju

_The five pages your site will have, and how they connect._

For a design agency, **showing your work is the single most powerful way to beat the "burned
by a bad freelancer" worry** — so a dedicated Work page earns its place. I've kept the proven
five-page shape and swapped the standalone FAQ for a Work page; the most useful questions get
answered in an FAQ *section* on the Home and Services pages (which still earns those handy
question-and-answer results in Google later).

## The five pages

```
Home (/)                         The pitch: who you help, the work, why you, → book a call
├── Work (/work)                 Portfolio — screenshots of apps & websites you've designed
├── Services (/services)         App design & web design, what's included, pricing/quote
├── About (/about)               Your story, 2+ years, how you work, why founders trust you
└── Contact (/contact)           Book a call (the main goal) + email & DMs
```

## What each page is for

| Page | Job it does | Main next step |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Home** | Hook the founder in 5 seconds, show proof, build trust | Book a call |
| **Work** | Prove quality with real screenshots — kill the "will it look pro?" fear | Book a call |
| **Services** | Explain app & web design clearly, set price expectations | Book a call |
| **About** | Make you human and trustworthy — speed, reliability, experience | Book a call |
| **Contact** | Make booking a call effortless | Book a call |

## Navigation

- **Top menu (every page):** Work · Services · About · Contact — plus a standout **Book a Call** button.
- **Logo** (top-left) links back to Home.
- **Footer (every page):** quick links to all pages, your email & social, and a final "Book a call".

## One clear action, everywhere

Every page guides the visitor to the same single step: **book a call.** No competing buttons,
no dead ends.
